## 1. Macro, Sector & Stock Cycle Context

### Broad Market — Step 1

Indian equity markets (Nifty 50) are in a **cautiously optimistic** phase as of May 2026, having recovered from the FII-driven correction of late 2025. Sentiment is neither euphoric nor fearful — broadly Neutral-to-Optimistic. India's structural investment story — capex-driven industrial growth, PLI schemes, infrastructure buildout — remains intact and is the dominant market narrative. VIX is at moderate levels. Sizing implication: standard position (not outsized), no need for defensive posture.

### Sector Cycle — Step 1b

**Assessment: Clear Tailwind | Duration: Secular, 5–7 years | Primary driver: India refinery + petchem capex mandate**

End-market demand for JNK India's products is primarily **secular**, not cyclical:

- India's installed refinery capacity stands at 258 MMT (April 2025) with a stated government target of 450–500 MMT by 2030 — a near-doubling. Every new refinery unit or upgrade requires fired heaters/furnaces.
- ₹3.28 lakh crore ($37 billion) in committed petrochemical capex to 2030 (Ministry of Petroleum/DPIIT).
- BPCL alone is investing ₹75,000 Cr in refinery expansion; HPCL, IOCL, and Reliance have parallel active programs.
- India's oil demand is projected at 5.74 mb/d in 2025 → 5.99 mb/d in 2026 (OPEC), supporting operating-refinery maintenance demand alongside greenfield.
- Natural gas consumption projected +60% by 2030 → fertilizer plant demand (JNK's ~0.2% today but a growing opportunity).

**Demand decomposition**: ~60–65% of observed demand growth is **secular** (mandated domestic refinery expansion, India petrochemical intensity goal). ~35–40% is **cyclical** (current oil-price-supportive capex cycle). Even in a flat-oil-price scenario, India's domestic demand-driven refinery buildout continues — this is the key durability argument.

**Input cost**: Material costs (steel, alloys, refractory) are 70–75% of revenue. No significant spike flagged in FY26 concalls. Global steel prices have been broadly stable-to-declining. A tailwind if input deflation continues.

**Competitive capacity**: No new domestic entrant has emerged in the full-range category. JNK India's Korean parent (JNK Global) continues to be the technology licensor. International OEMs (Heurtey Petrochem, John Zink) participate in large global projects but are not price-competitive for domestic Indian tenders at JNK's scale.

**Geopolitical**: Export revenues (Russia, Africa) carry moderate geopolitical risk. Russia-related business faces sanctions scrutiny. The higher-quality ₹4,000 Cr export pipeline in Middle East and Africa involves longer sales cycles but lower geopolitical risk. Net FX: export revenues likely USD-denominated; a stronger rupee scenario would compress reported INR realizations.

### Stock Cycle — Step 1c

**Assessment: Early-cycle ramp-up | FY27 is the BPCL Bina execution year**

Revenue growth is **accelerating**: FY25 flat (accounting-distorted) → FY26 +68% → FY27E +18–25% (analyst consensus). Q4 FY26 was the best quarter in company history (₹338 Cr revenue, 13.6% EBITDA margin, PAT ₹33 Cr). The order book at ₹1,961 Cr is 2.3× FY26 revenue — unprecedented coverage. FY26 order inflows totalled ₹1,694 Cr (+81% YoY), including the company's first large cracking furnace project.

The FY25 anomaly: Operating margin fell from 21% (FY24) to 10% (FY25), PAT from ₹62 Cr to ₹30 Cr. This was caused by an **accounting methodology change** — moving from output to input method for EPC revenue recognition (Ind-AS 115 compliance), which shifted timing of revenue recognition across quarters. It was NOT a structural deterioration. Q3–Q4 FY26 margins (10–14%) confirm recovery.

**Capacity cycle**: JNK India's model is primarily EPC (engineering, procurement, construction), not pure manufacturing. Physical capacity constraints lie in: (a) engineering bandwidth (skilled engineers for thermal design), (b) fabrication shop at Mundra (currently under-utilized for exports per Q3 FY26 concall), (c) project management capacity. No significant greenfield capex required — the ₹163 Cr investing outflow in FY26 is the JNK Chemdist JV, not core capacity.

**Management guidance accuracy**: Management guided for 13–16% EBITDA margins and ₹1,000 Cr+ revenue trajectory in FY27. The Q4 FY26 exit rate (13.6% EBITDA margin, ₹338 Cr quarterly run rate) supports the lower end of this range. Management has been conservative historically — the BPCL Bina 18-month timeline has not been revised.

**Working capital cycle**: CCC dramatically improved from 160 days (FY25) to 38 days (FY26) — the tightest since FY20. Debtor days fell from 261 to 139. This is the key positive signal in FY26 financials and suggests the chaos of FY25 was indeed a one-time impact.

**Implication**: Near-term (FY27–28) growth is well-supported by order book and BPCL Bina ramp. The terminal growth rate post-BPCL should moderate as that single project's contribution normalizes. Margin expansion from 11% to 14–16% is plausible but not a given.

Net: favorable for JNK India as long as it executes, but limits pricing upside.

## 3. Business Quality (Moat & Management)

**Moat: Narrow | Quality: Medium-High | Durability: Holding**

**Competitive advantages:**

1. **Technology + unique product scope**: India's only company producing all three product families — fired heaters, steam reformers, and cracking furnaces. Competitors (Thermax, Walchandnagar, Dee Development) offer subsets. The full-range capability means JNK can bid for comprehensive refinery packages, not just individual equipment lots. Technology license from JNK Global (Korea) underpins design capability.

2. **Customer relationships and repeat business**: Supplies 11 of 24 Indian operating refineries. Named clients include IOC, RCF, BPCL, HPCL. In EPC, proven track record is a strong repeat-business moat — a refinery operator will not risk a new vendor for critical heating equipment unless price differential is extreme.

3. **Efficient scale**: The Indian process heater market for large integrated orders supports 3–4 serious players. JNK's 27% share of new order bookings represents efficient scale — too large for a niche player, but in a market not large enough to support a third full-scale entrant easily.

**Management quality assessment:**
- Promoter holding stable at 67.80% over the past 1 year — no pledging reported.
- Arvind Kamath (Chairperson and WTD) has been with the business since inception (2010). Management's FY25 concall commentary acknowledging the methodology change, and the Q3/Q4 FY26 turnaround, reflects transparency.
- The JNK Chemdist JV (green hydrogen, August 2025) represents a forward-looking diversification bet, though ₹163 Cr was invested in FY26 with revenues 2+ years away. Capital allocation judgment here will only be visible in FY28–29.
- Management guided for "uniform execution to enhance cash flows and margins" in FY26 — the CCC improvement (160 → 38 days) validates this was not just talk.

**Customer concentration risk (most material qualitative concern):** BPCL Bina = ₹1,550 Cr / ₹1,961 Cr order book = **79% concentration in one project**. This is the dominant near-term risk and warrants a wider margin of safety than the moat quality alone implies.

**10-year moat durability**: Market leadership maintained throughout. Revenue CAGR 44% over 5 years with margin resilience (peak OPM 21%). ROCE declined from 70%+ pre-IPO to 17% post-IPO — the bulk of this is mathematical (4–5× equity base expansion from IPO proceeds and FY25 fundraise of ₹218 Cr), not structural moat erosion. Post-IPO sustainable ROCE at 15–20% is still above estimated WACC of 14%.

**TAM and market share ceiling**: The Indian fired heater/furnace market for new order bookings has been growing with the capex supercycle. JNK's 27% share has headroom to expand if it wins more export orders (currently 96% domestic order book). Export pipeline of ₹4,000 Cr represents a TAM expansion opportunity, not a saturated domestic position. No near-term market share ceiling.

## 10. Key Risks

1. **BPCL Bina concentration (Severe)**: ₹1,550 Cr = 79% of ₹1,961 Cr order book. This single PSU project controls the company's near-term destiny. A 6-month delay → FY27 revenue ₹800–900 Cr vs. expected ₹1,000+ Cr → 20–30% EPS miss → stock de-rating to 20× trough multiple → ₹220–260 price range. No comparable replacement order exists in the backfill pipeline.

2. **Margin vulnerability (Medium-High)**: Material costs at 70–75% of revenue. The FY25 collapse (OPM 21% → 10%) showed how quickly margins can compress. A commodity cost spike (steel, refractory alloys) or a shift to more complex/lower-margin cracking furnace projects could compress margins even as revenue grows.

3. **Cash flow structural lag (Medium)**: EPC businesses inherently absorb working capital during ramp phases. OCF/PAT was 0.31 in FY26. As BPCL Bina mobilizes, WC will expand again in FY27 before normalizing. FCF may remain below PAT until FY28.

4. **JNK Chemdist JV burn (Medium)**: ₹163 Cr invested in FY26 for green hydrogen/ethanol-to-chemicals JV. Revenue contribution expected "in 2 years" (Q3 FY26 concall). If hydrogen market development is slower than expected (which is the more likely scenario given current economics), this is effectively a ₹163 Cr write-down risk over a 3–4 year horizon.

5. **Institutional exit pressure (Low-Medium)**: DII + FII holdings have declined from ~22% to ~14% over FY26. As institutional holders reduce exposure, retail-dominated ownership increases stock volatility and reduces the quality of price discovery. Any further institutional block sale could cause a sharp short-term correction independent of fundamentals.

## 11. What Would Change This View

**Upgrade to Strong Setup:**
- Q1 FY27 revenue exceeds ₹300 Cr (confirms BPCL Bina execution pace) with EBITDA margin ≥12%
- Stock corrects to ₹295–310 and forms a 4–6 week consolidation base above ₹295
- First large export order from Middle East/Africa (>₹400 Cr single order) announced, reducing single-customer concentration below 60%
- Two consecutive quarters of OCF/PAT ratio above 0.60, confirming sustainable cash conversion

**Downgrade to Avoid:**
- BPCL Bina execution explicitly delayed beyond Q3 FY27 in management concall
- FY27 annual revenue guidance cut below ₹900 Cr
- OCF remains negative in FY27 despite higher revenue (structural WC deterioration signal)
- Promoter pledging appears in shareholding disclosures
- JNK Chemdist JV incurs material losses (>₹20 Cr annual loss) for two consecutive years
