## Verdict

**CHEAP BUT FLAWED**

Nupur Recyclers trades at a headline P/E of ~30× on thin ₹16 Cr net profits from ₹216 Cr revenue, which is not cheap on any absolute basis. The business has generated negative operating cash flow in three of its four listed years, margins are erratic, ROCE is declining, and the entire profit quality is undermined by outsized other-income (38.69% of PBT in Q3 FY26). The company is a small-scale import-and-trading operation with aspirational expansion stories (EV battery recycling, aluminium extrusion) that have not yet generated material revenue. At the current price it passes no margin of safety gate. The framework yields this verdict: **interesting sector, wrong vehicle at this price and quality level**.

## 1. Macro, Sector & Stock Cycle Context

### Broad market (Step 1)
Nifty 50 is at ~23,650 as of May 29, 2026, off its recent high of ~24,054. India VIX has eased to ~17.8–18.4, below its short-term moving averages, indicating moderating fear. FIIs turned net buyers in late April 2026 after three months of net selling, but the recovery is measured rather than aggressive. The market environment is best characterised as **Cautious-to-Neutral** — not euphorically priced, but not deeply fearful either. Position sizing should be conservative for speculative SME names.

### Sector cycle (Step 1b) — **TAILWIND**
India's non-ferrous scrap recycling market is projected to reach US$4.8 billion by 2030 (6.2% CAGR; Grand View Research). The structural drivers are genuine: rising per-capita copper and aluminium consumption, Government EV adoption mandates (30% private EV sales by 2030), Battery Waste Management Rules 2022 requiring 70–90% battery collection by 2026-27, and India's infrastructure spend creating demand for recycled aluminium and zinc. The EV battery recycling market alone is projected at US$463 million by 2030 (66.8% CAGR). Input cost cycle is mixed — global zinc and aluminium scrap prices are commodity-driven and volatile. **Primary driver of tailwind: regulatory mandates on circular economy and surging EV-battery-end-of-life volumes. Expected duration: multi-year structural (2026–2030+).** However, the tailwind benefits well-capitalised, integrated players (Gravita India, POCL) far more than a trading-heavy small-cap like NRL.

### Stock cycle position (Step 1c) — **Early cycle / ramp-up, unproven**
Revenue trajectory: ₹159 Cr (FY22) → ₹187 Cr (FY23) → ₹240 Cr (FY24) → ₹158 Cr (FY25) → ₹216 Cr (FY26). The -34% revenue collapse in FY25 followed by a +36% recovery in FY26 is not a controlled ramp — it looks like commodity cycle and import-volume volatility. The company is in an investment phase (subsidiary launches: Nupur Extrusion — ₹18 Cr capex into aluminium extrusion, Tycoon Autotech into auto-components from Sep 2025). Capacity is announced at 334,000 MTPA as of FY25. The stock cycle is better described as **speculative early stage** than a disciplined ramp-up.

## 2. Business Quality

**What NRL does**: Nupur Recyclers imports non-ferrous metal scrap — primarily shredded zinc scrap, zinc die-cast scrap, Zurik (zinc-rich) scrap, and Aluminium Zorba grades — and sells it on a B2B basis to Indian manufacturers who use it as feedstock. The company also processes and trades these materials. It operates facilities in Jaipur (including SEZ), Chittoor, Mundra (port-based), and Kathua, and has sourcing relationships in Ghana, Senegal, Mozambique, Tanzania, Sri Lanka, Oman, Dominican Republic, and Romania.

**Moat assessment: None.** This is fundamentally an import-and-distribution business. There are no patents, no proprietary technology, no switching costs, no network effects, and no cost advantage that is not replicable. The company's stated competitive differentiation — "long-term international supplier relationships" — is not a moat, it is a procurement relationship any well-capitalised competitor can replicate. Customer base is undisclosed in available sources (no customer concentration disclosure found), which is itself a governance flag.

**Expansion narrative**: NRL has announced moves into (a) aluminium extrusion (Nupur Extrusion Pvt Ltd subsidiary, ₹18 Cr capex, 5,000–6,000 MTPA facility in Haryana targeting solar and construction sectors), (b) lithium-ion battery recycling (facility planned for completion by 2027), (c) auto-components (Tycoon Autotech subsidiary, effective Sep 2025). These are optionalities, not revenue contributors at this time. Moving from scrap import/trading into battery recycling requires CPCB/state PCB authorisations, specialised pyrometallurgy/hydrometallurgy technology, and customer relationships with battery manufacturers — none of which NRL has demonstrated it possesses.

**Management quality**: Promoters are Rajesh Gupta and Anoop Garg (principal promoters). The company is only 7 years old (incorporated 2019), listed in 2021. Management guidance track record cannot be assessed over a long history. The FY25 revenue collapse (-34% YoY) was not adequately explained in available disclosures. No concall transcripts are publicly accessible via free sources. Related-party transactions are not quantified in available sources. **Capital allocation quality score: Insufficient history; early flags are negative** (mounting debt, negative OCF, subsidiary proliferation into unrelated areas within 5 years of founding).

**What the data reveals**:

1. **ROCE trajectory is deteriorating**: From 19% in FY24 to ~11% in FY26, despite revenue recovering. This is happening while debt is rising — a classic sign that incremental capital is being deployed at sub-CoC returns.

2. **Three consecutive years of negative OCF (FY23–FY25)**: A business reporting ₹15–16 Cr in net profit but burning ₹11–19 Cr in operating cash flow every year is either building working capital aggressively (possible in a trading business with import cycles) or has earnings quality issues. FY26 finally showed +₹18 Cr OCF — one positive year is insufficient to call this resolved.

## 4. Earnings Quality

**Assessment: CONCERNING.**

Three specific red flags:

1. **Other income = 38.69% of PBT in Q3 FY26** (₹2.55 Cr of ₹6.59 Cr PBT). This is the single most serious near-term red flag. When nearly 40% of pre-tax profit comes from non-operating income in a trading company, reported PAT is not a reliable indicator of operating health. Source: MarketsMojo Q3 FY26 analysis.

2. **Chronic negative OCF vs. positive net income (FY23–FY25)**: Three consecutive years where reported net income was positive (₹9–16 Cr) but operating cash flow was deeply negative (-₹11 to -₹19 Cr). This gap is the textbook Schilit warning sign. The FY26 OCF recovery (+₹18 Cr) needs to be sustained over at least 2–3 more years before it can be trusted.

3. **Opaque disclosures**: Customer concentration is not disclosed. Concall transcripts are not publicly available on free platforms. Related-party transaction quantum not quantified in available sources. For a listed SME company, this level of disclosure opacity is a governance concern.

**What was not found**: No qualified audit opinion, no auditor change, no SEBI notice found in available public sources. Promoter pledging not disclosed or confirmed as zero.

## 5. Valuation

### 5a. Price-Implied Expectations (Mauboussin)

**Back-solving PIE** at current EV (Market cap ₹450 Cr + Debt ₹33 Cr – Cash est. ₹10 Cr = EV ~₹473 Cr), LTM EBIT ~₹17 Cr, NOPAT ~₹13 Cr (at 25% effective tax), WACC ~14% (10-yr G-sec ~7.2% + beta ~1.2 × 6% ERP):

Using the perpetuity formula: g = (EV × WACC − NOPAT) / (EV + NOPAT) = (473 × 0.14 − 13) / (473 + 13) = (66.2 − 13) / 486 = **10.9% implied perpetual growth rate**.

For a two-stage model (5-yr explicit + terminal at 5%): back-solving implies **~18–22% revenue CAGR** over 5 years with NOPAT margins of ~7–8% — which requires the company to scale from ₹216 Cr to ~₹490–520 Cr in revenue by FY31, sustain margins above current levels, and convert earnings to cash consistently.

**Expectations assessment**: The market is pricing in an aggressive growth + margin improvement scenario that the company's track record does not support. The most dangerous assumption is OCF conversion — three years of negative OCF means the market is extrapolating FY26's single good year into perpetuity.

**Expectations treadmill**: For NRL to merely earn its cost of capital (14%), it must sustain ~11% perpetual revenue growth with ~7% NOPAT margins and convert >100% of net income to OCF — none of which it has demonstrated consistently.

**Positive triggers**: (1) Q1–Q2 FY27 OCF confirms FY26 was not a one-off; (2) aluminium extrusion subsidiary generates material revenue (₹30+ Cr) in FY27; (3) CPCB authorisation received for battery recycling facility.

**Negative triggers**: (1) Q1 FY27 OCF turns negative again; (2) debt rises above ₹50 Cr without corresponding revenue growth; (3) other-income contribution to PBT remains above 30%; (4) FY27 revenue growth disappoints below 15%.

**SVAR**: Downside scenario — 10% revenue CAGR, 5% NOPAT margin, OCF/NI of 0.6×: implied EV ~₹130–150 Cr → implied equity value ~₹100–120 Cr → implied price ~₹14–17 per share. **SVAR = ~74–79% from current price of ₹65**. Asymmetry is deeply **Unfavorable**.

## 7. Key Risks

1. **Earnings quality collapse**: If other income (38.69% of PBT) normalises to 5–10%, reported PAT halves immediately without any change in operations. This is the most proximate risk.

2. **Working capital spiral**: Import-trading businesses are vulnerable to commodity price moves and credit cycles. Three consecutive years of negative OCF suggest the working capital model may be structurally cash-consumptive, especially as the company scales imports.

3. **Subsidiary over-reach**: Three subsidiaries (extrusion, battery recycling, auto-components) within 5 years of founding, in industries with high capital intensity and regulatory requirements, spread management bandwidth and capital across areas where NRL has no demonstrated expertise. Gravita and POCL took decades to build the capabilities NRL is attempting to acquire in 2–3 years.

4. **Regulatory / CPCB risk for battery recycling**: Lithium-ion battery recycling in India requires CPCB hazardous waste authorisation, State PCB approvals, and compliance with Battery Waste Management Rules 2022. NRL has not disclosed having these approvals. The 2027 completion timeline for the battery facility is highly uncertain.

5. **Commodity price and import policy risk**: NRL's margins are exposed to global zinc/aluminium scrap prices, USD/INR moves, and any change in India's import duty on non-ferrous scrap (currently 0–2.5% basic customs duty, but policy changes are possible). Net FX exposure is structurally long-USD (imports priced in USD, revenue in INR) — a 5% INR depreciation adds ~₹8–10 Cr to costs on the current import base, which would wipe ~60–70% of annual PAT.

## 8. What Would Change This View

**Verdict would upgrade to "Wait for Confirmation"** if: (a) Q1 and Q2 FY27 both show OCF > ₹10 Cr with other-income < 15% of PBT; (b) aluminium extrusion subsidiary posts ₹25+ Cr revenue in FY27 at EBITDA margin > 10%; (c) ROCE stabilises and shows a genuine uptick above 15%; and (d) price corrects to ₹25–30 range, creating genuine margin of safety.

**Verdict would upgrade to "Strong Setup"** only if: All of the above plus CPCB/PCB authorisations confirmed for battery recycling, FY27 consolidated EBITDA reaches ₹30+ Cr with clean OCF conversion > 80% of net profit, and stock builds a 12-week+ base above ₹28–30 with expanding volume.

**Verdict would downgrade to "Avoid"** if: Next 2 quarters show OCF turning negative again, or debt crosses ₹60 Cr without corresponding EBITDA growth, or auditor flags related-party concern.
